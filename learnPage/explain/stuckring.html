Removing a Stuck Ring

Safely relieve pressure and remove a stuck ring using the thread method

Introduction

A stuck ring can happen for a variety of reasons, including sudden weight gain, pregnancy, hot weather causing swelling, or an injury to the hand or finger.

If a ring becomes too tight, it can act like a tourniquet, cutting off blood circulation to the finger. This can lead to severe pain, tissue damage, and even permanent nerve injury if left untreated. Knowing how to safely remove it using household items like dental floss or thread is an essential first aid skill.

First Aid: The Thread Method

If simple twisting and pulling doesn't work, follow these exact steps to use the thread (or dental floss) method:

  1. Insert the string: Insert the end of a thin thread or dental floss carefully under and down the ring toward the hand. (You can use a toothpick or safety pin to gently guide it under if it's very tight).
  2. Lubricate: Use cream, soap, or oil to thoroughly grease the thread, the ring, and the ring finger.
  3. Wrap the finger: Pull the other end of the string (the side pointing toward the fingertip) and wrap it tightly around the finger. Make sure that the thread has been wrapped regularly and smoothly all the way past the bottom of the finger joint (the knuckle).
  4. Unwind: Catch the end of the string that was entered under the ring (the side pointing toward the hand), and start releasing the thread in the same direction you wrapped it. As you unwrap, the ring should slowly move up the thread and over the knuckle.

When to Seek Emergency Care

Do not force the ring if the finger is severely compromised. Stop your attempts and go immediately to the emergency department of the nearest hospital so they can safely cut the ring off if:

  • There is severe, rapid swelling that makes the thread method impossible.
  • It is not possible to take out the ring after trying the thread method.
  • The finger turns blue, purple, or pale white (indicating loss of blood flow).
  • The finger goes completely numb or cold to the touch.
  • The ring is stuck due to a traumatic injury, fracture, or deep cut on the hand.
Emergency Note: Hospitals and fire departments have specialized ring cutters that can safely slice through the metal without harming the finger. Your health and finger are more important than the jewelry.

Stuck Ring DOs and DON'Ts

✔ DOs

  • Elevate the hand above your heart for 5-10 minutes before trying to remove the ring to reduce swelling.
  • Apply ice to the finger to help shrink the blood vessels.
  • Use plenty of lubrication (dish soap, baby oil, or hand cream).
  • Use strong, thin string like dental floss or ribbon.
  • Go to the ER if you lose feeling in your finger.

✖ DON'Ts

  • Do NOT yank or pull forcefully on the ring (this causes the joint to swell more).
  • Do NOT use harsh chemicals to try and dissolve swelling.
  • Do NOT leave the thread wrapped tightly around the finger for more than a minute or two.
  • Do NOT try to cut the ring off yourself with household tools like pliers or wire cutters.

Prevention Tips

🏃

Remove rings before heavy workouts.

🤰

Take off rings during late pregnancy.

✈️

Remove rings on long flights.

🧼

Clean rings to prevent buildup.

📏

Have rings resized if they feel tight.

🧊

Cool your hands in hot weather.
