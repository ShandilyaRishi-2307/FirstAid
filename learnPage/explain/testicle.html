Testicle Injuries

Immediate first aid steps, pain management, and knowing when to seek help

Introduction

Testicular injuries most commonly occur due to blunt force trauma, such as a kick, a sports collision, or an accident. Because the testicles have a high number of sensitive nerve endings, even a minor impact can cause intense, temporary pain, nausea, and discomfort.

While many minor injuries can be managed with simple first aid at home, it is crucial to monitor the injury closely. Severe impacts can lead to medical emergencies like testicular rupture or torsion, which require urgent medical intervention to save the testicle.

Immediate First Aid Steps

If someone experiences a sudden injury to the testicles, follow these steps to manage the pain and swelling:

  1. Calm and Reassure: Calm and reassure the casualty. The pain can be severe and cause anxiety or nausea, so breathing slowly is important.
  2. Apply Cold Compress: Apply an ice pack or cold compress to the skin of the testicle to lessen swelling. Crucial: Direct contact between the ice and scrotum should be avoided; always wrap it in a cloth.
  3. Provide Pain Relief: Assuming that the casualty has no allergic reaction or medical contraindications, over-the-counter pain killers may be given to reduce pain.
  4. Elevate and Support: Raise the scrotum and observe for pain relief. If elevation helps relieve the ache, try having the person wear tight, supportive briefs or an athletic supporter, which can help keep the testicles raised.

Things to Remember

Monitoring the long-term health of the testicles is vital. Keep these key points in mind:

  • Seek Urgent Advice: If you injure your testicles (during a sport, an assault, or a vehicle accident, for example), always seek urgent medical advice to rule out severe internal damage.
  • Self-Examination: Perform testicular self-examination (TSE) regularly to familiarize yourself with what is normal for your body.
  • Consult a Doctor: See your doctor immediately if you experience any persistent pain, unusual symptoms, or if you discover a lump or abnormal swelling.
Emergency Warning: If the pain is sudden, severe, and does not go away within an hour, or if it is accompanied by blood in the urine, fever, or extreme swelling, go to the nearest emergency department immediately. This could be a sign of testicular torsion or rupture.

Testicle Injury DOs and DON'Ts

✔ DOs

  • Rest quietly and allow the initial severe pain to pass.
  • Apply ice wrapped in a towel for 10-15 minutes at a time.
  • Wear supportive underwear (like briefs instead of boxers) to elevate the scrotum.
  • Take an over-the-counter pain reliever if safe to do so.
  • Go to a doctor if the pain persists for more than an hour.

✖ DON'Ts

  • Do NOT apply ice or frozen items directly to the bare skin.
  • Do NOT ignore long-lasting pain, nausea, or rapid swelling.
  • Do NOT resume vigorous sports or physical activity immediately after the injury.
  • Do NOT hesitate to perform self-exams regularly after you have healed.

Prevention and Health Tips

🛡️

Wear an athletic cup during contact sports.

🚴

Ensure a proper bike seat fit to avoid repetitive trauma.

🩲

Wear supportive underwear during workouts.

🚗

Always wear your seatbelt while driving.

🔍

Perform regular testicular self-exams.

👨‍⚕️

Report any new lumps to a doctor.
