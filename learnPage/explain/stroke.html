Stroke

Act FAST – Every Minute Counts

Introduction

A stroke occurs when the blood supply to part of the brain is interrupted due to a blood clot or bleeding inside the brain. Without oxygen and nutrients, brain cells begin to die within minutes. Because the brain controls movement, speech, memory and many body functions, a stroke can cause permanent disability or even death.

A stroke is a medical emergency. Rapid recognition and treatment can greatly reduce brain damage and improve recovery. Knowing the warning signs and acting immediately can save a life.

Types of Stroke

  • Ischemic Stroke: Caused by a blood clot blocking blood flow to the brain.
  • Hemorrhagic Stroke: Caused by bleeding from a ruptured blood vessel.
  • Transient Ischemic Attack (TIA): A temporary "mini-stroke" warning sign.

Risk Factors

  • High blood pressure.
  • Diabetes.
  • Smoking and alcohol abuse.
  • High cholesterol.
  • Heart disease.
  • Obesity and lack of exercise.
  • Family history of stroke.
  • Increasing age.

FAST Test

  • F – Face: Ask the person to smile. Is one side drooping?
  • A – Arms: Ask them to raise both arms. Does one arm drift downward?
  • S – Speech: Is speech slurred or difficult?
  • T – Time: Call emergency services immediately.

Other Warning Signs

  • Sudden weakness or numbness.
  • Loss of balance or coordination.
  • Severe headache.
  • Blurred or double vision.
  • Confusion.
  • Dizziness.
  • Difficulty swallowing.
  • Loss of consciousness.
Important: Even if symptoms disappear quickly, seek emergency medical care immediately.

First Aid for Stroke

  1. Call emergency medical services immediately.
  2. Help the person sit or lie comfortably.
  3. Keep them calm and reassured.
  4. Loosen tight clothing.
  5. Monitor breathing, pulse and responsiveness.
  6. If unconscious but breathing, place in the recovery position.
  7. If breathing stops, begin CPR.
  8. Note the time symptoms started and inform medical personnel.

Things You Should Avoid

  • Do not give food or drinks.
  • Do not give medication unless prescribed.
  • Do not allow the person to walk around.
  • Do not delay calling emergency services.
  • Do not shake or move the person unnecessarily.
  • Do not leave the person alone.

Stroke First Aid

✔ DOs

  • Use the FAST test.
  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • Keep the person comfortable.
  • Monitor breathing and pulse.
  • Place unconscious casualties in the recovery position.
  • Start CPR if breathing stops.

✖ DON'Ts

  • Do not give food or water.
  • Do not allow smoking or alcohol.
  • Do not let the person drive.
  • Do not ignore mild symptoms.
  • Do not leave the casualty alone.
  • Do not delay treatment.

Possible Complications

A stroke may cause paralysis, difficulty speaking, memory problems, swallowing difficulties, vision loss, emotional disturbances, seizures, and permanent disability. Severe strokes can result in coma or death.

Prompt medical treatment greatly improves recovery and reduces long-term complications.

Prevention and Healthy Habits

🥗

Eat a healthy diet.

🏃

Exercise regularly.

🚭

Avoid smoking.

💧

Stay hydrated.

🩺

Control blood pressure and diabetes.

❤️

Maintain heart health.

Why Immediate Treatment Matters

Brain cells begin to die within minutes when blood supply is interrupted. Early treatment can restore blood flow and minimize brain damage. The sooner treatment begins, the better the chances of survival and recovery.

Remember the word FAST: Face, Arms, Speech, Time. Recognizing these signs quickly and calling emergency services immediately can save lives and reduce disability.
