Broken or Knocked-Out Teeth

Protect the tooth quickly and seek emergency dental care

Introduction

A knocked-out permanent tooth is a dental emergency. Fast action greatly improves the chance of saving the tooth. Teeth that are reinserted within 30 minutes have the highest chance of successful re-implantation.

Broken, chipped, or completely avulsed teeth can occur after sports injuries, falls, road accidents, or blows to the face. Proper handling of the tooth and prompt dental treatment can help prevent permanent tooth loss and complications.

Common Causes

  • Falls and slips.
  • Sports injuries.
  • Road traffic accidents.
  • Physical fights or trauma.
  • Biting hard objects.
  • Workplace accidents.
  • Weak teeth caused by decay.

Signs and Symptoms

  • Missing or loose tooth.
  • Pain and sensitivity.
  • Bleeding from the gums.
  • Swelling around the mouth.
  • Difficulty biting or chewing.
  • Visible cracks or broken fragments.
  • Bruising around lips or gums.

Danger Signs

  • Severe bleeding.
  • Jaw injury or fracture.
  • Difficulty breathing.
  • Loss of consciousness after injury.
  • Multiple knocked-out teeth.
  • Severe facial swelling.
Emergency: A permanent tooth should ideally be reinserted or preserved and evaluated by a dentist immediately.

Immediate First Aid Steps

  1. Locate the tooth or fragments.
  2. Touch only the crown and avoid touching the root.
  3. Rinse gently in lukewarm water for less than 10 seconds if dirty.
  4. Do not scrub or use soap or alcohol.
  5. Rinse the mouth with warm water.
  6. If possible, place the permanent tooth back into the socket.
  7. Ask the person to bite gently on gauze to hold it in place.
  8. If reimplantation is impossible, place the tooth in milk or inside the cheek.
  9. Control bleeding using sterile gauze.
  10. Apply a cold compress to reduce swelling.
  11. Seek emergency dental treatment immediately.

Tooth Injury DOs and DON'Ts

✔ DOs

  • Handle the tooth carefully.
  • Hold it by the crown only.
  • Keep the tooth moist.
  • Use milk if transport is needed.
  • Apply cold compresses.
  • Visit a dentist immediately.

✖ DON'Ts

  • Do not touch the root.
  • Do not scrub the tooth.
  • Do not let the tooth dry out.
  • Do not delay treatment.
  • Do not store it in plain water for long periods.
  • Do not ignore facial injuries.

Possible Complications

Without prompt treatment, a knocked-out tooth may not survive. Infection, gum damage, nerve injury, abscess formation, and permanent tooth loss can occur. Delayed treatment can also affect chewing, speech, and appearance.

In severe trauma, fractures of the jaw or surrounding facial bones may accompany dental injuries and require emergency medical attention.

Prevention and Safety Tips

🥽

Wear mouthguards during sports.

🚲

Use helmets while cycling.

🍬

Avoid biting hard objects.

🦷

Maintain dental hygiene.

👨‍⚕️

Attend regular dental checkups.

⚽

Use protective sports equipment.

Why Early Treatment Matters

The success of reimplanting a permanent tooth depends mainly on how quickly treatment is started. Teeth replaced within 30 minutes have the best chance of survival. Proper storage and immediate dental care help preserve the root and supporting tissues.

Even if the tooth has been successfully repositioned, follow-up treatment by a dentist is essential to prevent infection and maintain long-term function.
