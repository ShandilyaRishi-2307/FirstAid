Heart Attack

Immediate action can save a life

Introduction

A heart attack occurs when blood flow to a part of the heart is blocked, preventing oxygen from reaching the heart muscle. Without oxygen, heart tissue begins to die. Immediate treatment is essential because delayed care can lead to severe damage or death.

Recognizing symptoms early and providing prompt first aid greatly improves the chance of survival. Heart attacks can affect people of all ages but are more common in individuals with high blood pressure, diabetes, smoking habits, obesity, and high cholesterol.

Common Causes and Risk Factors

  • Coronary artery blockage due to cholesterol buildup.
  • High blood pressure.
  • Smoking and tobacco use.
  • Diabetes.
  • Obesity and lack of exercise.
  • Stress and anxiety.
  • Family history of heart disease.
  • Excessive alcohol consumption.

Signs and Symptoms

  • Chest pain or pressure.
  • Pain spreading to arms, shoulders, neck or jaw.
  • Shortness of breath.
  • Cold sweat.
  • Nausea or vomiting.
  • Dizziness or fainting.
  • Weakness and unusual fatigue.
  • Rapid or irregular heartbeat.

Important Facts

Heart attack symptoms may appear hours, days or weeks before the attack. Some people, especially women, elderly adults and diabetics, may experience mild or unusual symptoms.

Important: Chest pain lasting more than 15 minutes should never be ignored. Seek emergency medical help immediately.

First Aid During a Heart Attack

  1. Call emergency medical services immediately.
  2. Help the person sit comfortably and stay calm.
  3. Loosen tight clothing.
  4. Give aspirin to chew if not allergic and advised by a doctor.
  5. Assist with prescribed nitroglycerin medication.
  6. Monitor breathing and responsiveness.
  7. Prepare to start CPR if the person becomes unconscious.
  8. Use an AED if available.

Heart Attack First Aid

✔ DOs

  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • Keep the patient calm.
  • Allow the person to sit and rest.
  • Give prescribed nitroglycerin.
  • Start CPR if breathing stops.
  • Use an AED if available.

✖ DON'Ts

  • Do not ignore chest pain.
  • Do not allow unnecessary walking.
  • Do not give food or drinks.
  • Do not leave the person alone.
  • Do not delay calling emergency services.
  • Do not give medication not prescribed to the patient.

Why Quick Treatment Matters

Heart attacks can cause severe complications including heart failure, irregular heartbeat, cardiac arrest, and permanent heart muscle damage. Early treatment with medications, CPR and defibrillation greatly increases survival chances.

Learning CPR and knowing how to use an Automated External Defibrillator (AED) can save lives before professional medical help arrives.

Prevention is the Best Protection

Exercise regularly.

Eat a heart-healthy diet.

Avoid smoking.

Control blood pressure.

Manage diabetes and cholesterol.

Learn CPR and first aid.
