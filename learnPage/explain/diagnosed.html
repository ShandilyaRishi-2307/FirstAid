Diagnosed Case of Death

Recognizing signs and respecting emergency procedures

Introduction

Death occurs when breathing, blood circulation and brain function permanently stop. In emergency situations, absence of pulse and breathing despite prolonged CPR attempts may indicate death. Certain physical signs can further support this conclusion.

Legal confirmation of death should always be performed by trained healthcare professionals. Emergency services should still be contacted whenever possible.

Confirmed Signs of Death

  • No pulse and no breathing for at least 15 minutes despite CPR attempts.
  • Pupils remain fixed and do not react to light.
  • Body becomes cold and pale.
  • Lower jaw becomes relaxed and hangs open.
  • White foam or fluids may appear around the mouth.
  • Cloudy eyes.
  • Rigor mortis (stiffening of muscles).
  • Livor mortis (purple discoloration of skin).
Important: Only qualified healthcare professionals can officially certify death.

What To Do

  1. Call emergency medical services immediately.
  2. Check pulse and breathing.
  3. Begin CPR if appropriate and trained.
  4. Continue resuscitation until professionals arrive unless obvious irreversible signs are present.
  5. Keep the surroundings calm and safe.
  6. Avoid unnecessary movement of the body.
  7. Provide information to emergency responders.
  8. Support family members and witnesses.

Common Causes

  • Cardiac arrest.
  • Stroke.
  • Respiratory failure.
  • Drowning.
  • Severe trauma.
  • Drug overdose.
  • Massive blood loss.
  • Terminal illness.

Physical Changes After Death

  • Cooling of the body.
  • Pale skin.
  • Fixed pupils.
  • Jaw relaxation.
  • Muscle stiffness.
  • Purple discoloration of the skin.
  • Clouding of the eyes.

Emergency Guidelines

✔ DOs

  • Call emergency services.
  • Follow local emergency protocols.
  • Remain calm.
  • Continue CPR if appropriate.
  • Protect the dignity of the person.
  • Cooperate with healthcare professionals.

✖ DON'Ts

  • Do not put fingers into the throat.
  • Do not assume death without proper assessment.
  • Do not panic.
  • Do not disturb the surroundings unnecessarily.
  • Do not leave the person unattended.
  • Do not delay seeking professional help.

Why Immediate Action Matters

Cardiac arrest and death are not always the same. Many people who initially appear lifeless can be revived with prompt CPR and emergency treatment. Early action greatly improves survival.

When death is irreversible, emergency personnel and healthcare providers will provide guidance and support to family members while ensuring dignity and proper care.
