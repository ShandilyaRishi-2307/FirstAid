Eye Injuries

Protect vision and seek prompt medical care

Introduction

Eye injuries range from minor irritation caused by dust or shampoo to severe injuries involving cuts, bruises, or penetrating wounds that can permanently affect eyesight. Even seemingly small scratches on the surface of the eye can lead to infection or scarring if not treated properly.

Foreign objects such as eyelashes, grit, wood, or metal fragments commonly enter the eye. Severe blows from sports injuries, falls, or accidents may also damage delicate eye structures. Every eye injury should be treated seriously because vision loss can occur without prompt care.

Common Causes

  • Dust, sand, or grit entering the eye.
  • Metal, wood, or glass fragments.
  • Sports injuries from balls or other objects.
  • Cuts caused by sharp objects.
  • Chemical splashes and cleaning products.
  • Road traffic accidents.
  • Falls and facial trauma.

Signs and Symptoms

  • Pain in the eye or eyelid.
  • Redness or bloodshot appearance.
  • Excessive watering.
  • Sensitivity to light.
  • Blurred or reduced vision.
  • Difficulty opening the eye.
  • Feeling that something is stuck inside.
  • Bruising or swelling around the eye.

Danger Signs

  • Partial or complete loss of vision.
  • Blood or clear fluid leaking from the eye.
  • Deep cuts or puncture wounds.
  • Severe pain or persistent irritation.
  • Visible foreign body embedded in the eye.
  • Unequal pupil size.
  • Double vision.
Emergency: All serious eye injuries require urgent medical attention because permanent vision loss can occur.

Immediate First Aid Steps

  1. Tell the person not to rub the eye.
  2. Ask them to sit facing a good light source.
  3. Gently examine the eye while they look up, down, left, and right.
  4. If loose particles are present, rinse the eye with clean water.
  5. If discomfort remains, seek medical care immediately.
  6. For bruised or cut eyes, help the person lie down.
  7. Keep the head and both eyes as still as possible.
  8. Cover the injured eye with a sterile dressing or clean pad.
  9. Secure the dressing lightly with a bandage if necessary.
  10. Take or send the person to hospital immediately.

Eye Injury DOs and DON'Ts

✔ DOs

  • Wash out loose particles with clean water.
  • Keep the injured eye protected.
  • Seek medical attention promptly.
  • Keep both eyes as still as possible.
  • Use a sterile dressing when available.
  • Wear protective eyewear during risky activities.

✖ DON'Ts

  • Do not rub the eye.
  • Do not try to remove embedded objects.
  • Do not apply pressure to the eye.
  • Do not use tweezers on deep foreign bodies.
  • Do not allow unnecessary eye movement.
  • Do not delay seeking medical help.

Possible Complications

Untreated eye injuries can result in infection, corneal scarring, retinal damage, bleeding inside the eye, glaucoma, and permanent loss of vision. Deep injuries may also damage surrounding tissues and require emergency surgery.

Prompt treatment significantly improves the chances of preserving eyesight and preventing long-term complications.

Prevention and Safety Tips

🥽

Wear protective goggles.

⚽

Use sports eye protection.

🧪

Handle chemicals carefully.

🛠️

Use safety equipment at work.

🚗

Wear seat belts in vehicles.

👨‍⚕️

Get regular eye examinations.

Why Early Treatment Matters

The eyes are delicate organs, and even minor injuries can rapidly become serious if ignored. Immediate first aid and prompt medical treatment help prevent infection, scarring, and permanent damage to vision.

Early intervention greatly improves recovery and preserves sight. Delaying treatment may result in irreversible vision loss and other long-term complications.
