Animal Bites

Control bleeding, clean the wound, and seek medical help to prevent infection

Introduction

Animal bites—whether from a domestic pet like a dog or cat, or a wild or stray animal—need to be taken seriously. Even a bite that appears to be a minor scratch can introduce dangerous bacteria into the skin, leading to severe infections.

Additionally, bites from unvaccinated pets or wild animals carry a high risk of transmitting the rabies virus, which is fatal if left untreated. Knowing how to properly administer first aid and when to seek emergency medical care is crucial to a safe recovery.

When to Call Emergency Services

Call for immediate emergency medical assistance (like 911) if any of the following apply:

  • Serious Wound: The person has been seriously wounded or tissues are severely torn.
  • Unstoppable Bleeding: Bleeding cannot be stopped after 10 minutes of firm and steady pressure.
  • Severe Bleeding: The wound is bleeding heavily or pooling rapidly.
  • Spurting Blood: Blood spurts from the wound, which indicates an arterial injury.
Important: Keep the victim calm and continue applying direct pressure to the wound with a clean cloth while waiting for emergency responders to arrive.

Immediate First Aid

For a wound or superficial scratch from an animal bite, follow these steps:

  1. Stop Bleeding: Apply direct pressure to the wound with a clean cloth or sterile bandage until the bleeding stops.
  2. Clean Thoroughly: Gently clean the wound with soap and warm water. Rinse the area under running water for several minutes after washing.
  3. Protect the Wound: Apply an over-the-counter antibiotic cream to reduce the risk of infection.
  4. Bandage: Cover the wound with a clean, sterile bandage.

Getting Help

Always take precautions regarding infection and rabies:

  • Seek Medical Care: Get medical help immediately for any animal bite that is more than a superficial scratch, or if the animal was a wild animal or stray, regardless of how minor the injury seems.
  • Check Vaccination Status: If the animal's owner is available, find out if the animal's rabies shots are up-to-date. Give this exact information to your health care provider.
  • Report the Animal: If the animal was a stray or wild animal, call the local health department or animal control immediately so the animal can be safely captured and observed.

Animal Bite DOs and DON'Ts

✔ DOs

  • Wash the wound immediately with plenty of soap and water.
  • Apply firm, steady pressure to stop bleeding.
  • Verify the animal's rabies vaccination status if possible.
  • Report stray or wild animal bites to animal control.
  • Seek medical attention for deep puncture wounds.

✖ DON'Ts

  • Do not ignore a bite from a wild or stray animal.
  • Do not try to capture or provoke the wild animal yourself.
  • Do not leave the wound unwashed or uncovered.
  • Do not delay getting a tetanus shot if you are due for one.
  • Do not assume a cat bite is harmless (they have a high infection rate).

Medical Treatment & Follow Up

When you visit a health care provider for an animal bite, here is what you can expect:

  • Wound Cleaning: The provider will ensure the wound is thoroughly cleaned and may prescribe oral antibiotics to prevent infection.
  • Deep Examination: The health care provider may numb the wound area and examine it closely to look for deeper damage to nerves, tendons, or bones.
  • Rabies Treatment: If there is any risk of a rabies infection, the provider will recommend a series of anti-rabies treatments (post-exposure prophylaxis).
  • Stitches: The person may require stitches to close the wound, depending on how big the wound is and its location (though some puncture wounds are left open to heal and drain).
  • Tetanus Booster: The person may require a tetanus shot or booster if it has been more than 5-10 years since their last one.
  • Pain Management: The health care provider may recommend ibuprofen or acetaminophen for pain relief.

Prevention and Safety Tips

🐕

Always ask the owner before petting a dog.

💤

Never disturb an animal that is sleeping.

🍽️

Do not bother animals while they are eating.

🦊

Leave wildlife and stray animals alone.

💉

Keep your own pets vaccinated.

👶

Supervise young children around pets.
