Strains and Sprains

Master the R.I.C.E method for quick and effective recovery

Introduction

Strains and sprains are common injuries which affect the soft tissues around joints – the muscles, tendons, and ligaments. They happen when the tissues are stretched, twisted, or torn by violent or sudden movements, for instance, if someone changes direction suddenly, or falls and lands awkwardly.

What's the difference?
• A sprain is when a ligament has been twisted or torn.
• A strain is when the muscle has been overstretched and has partially torn. (A rupture is when a muscle or tendon is completely torn).

What to Look For

If you think someone may have strained or sprained a muscle, ligament, or tendon, these are the three key things to look for:

  • Pain and tenderness: The affected area will hurt, especially when touched or moved.
  • Difficulty moving: They may not be able to put weight on the limb or move the joint properly.
  • Swelling and bruising: The area may puff up and discolor rapidly after the injury occurs.

Treatment: The R.I.C.E. Method

Remember RICE for the four steps to deal with strains and sprains.

1. Rest

Help the casualty to sit or lie down and support the part they’ve hurt in a comfortable, raised position. Prevent them from putting any weight on the injured area.

2. Ice

To cool the area, apply a cold compress, like an ice pack or cold pad. This will help to reduce the swelling, bruising, and pain. Do not leave on for more than 10 minutes.

3. Comfortable Support

Leave the cold compress in place or wrap a soft layer of padding, e.g., cotton wool, around the area. Tie a support bandage around it to hold it in place. It should go as far as the next joint on each side (e.g., from base of toes to knees for an ankle).

4. Elevation

Elevate the injury and support it with something soft, like cushions. This reduces blood flow to the area, limiting swelling.

When to seek hospital care: If the pain is severe, or they can’t use their limb at all, take or send them to the hospital. Otherwise, just tell them to rest it and to see a health care professional if necessary.

Strains & Sprains DOs and DON'Ts

✔ DOs

  • Follow the R.I.C.E. method immediately.
  • Wrap ice in a towel to prevent ice burns.
  • Elevate the limb above the level of the heart if possible.
  • Use pillows or cushions for comfortable elevation.
  • Check circulation after bandaging (press a nail and see if color returns).

✖ DON'Ts

  • Do not apply heat (like hot baths or heat packs) in the first 48 hours.
  • Do not apply ice directly to the bare skin.
  • Do not massage the injured area right away.
  • Do not wrap the support bandage too tightly (it will restrict blood flow).
  • Do not attempt to push through the pain.
