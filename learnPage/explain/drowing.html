Drownings

Quick action can save a life

Introduction

Instant first aid should be given to a person rescued from drowning, along with immediate medical attention. Every second counts and prompt action can save a life.

Causes of Drowning Accidents

  • Seizures or blows to the head under water.
  • Attempt to commit suicide.
  • Falling through thin ice.
  • Alcohol consumption while swimming or boating.
  • Unattended children around pools and bathtubs.
  • Panic while swimming or inability to swim.

Facts Related to Drowning

Thousands of drowning incidents occur every year. Most happen close to safety zones. Children can drown in only a few inches of water. Young victims submerged in cold water for long periods can sometimes be revived.

Important: Look for unusual swimming motions and signs of exhaustion. Check if the victim is fully clothed, which may indicate accidental drowning.

Symptoms of Near Drowning

  • Vomiting
  • Restlessness
  • Confusion
  • Chest pain
  • Pale appearance
  • Cold skin
  • Bluish lips or skin
  • Abdominal distention
  • Unconsciousness
  • Lack of breathing
  • Lethargy
  • Coughing froth

How to Bring a Drowning Victim to Land

  1. Wear a flotation device if available.
  2. Call emergency services immediately.
  3. Reach or pull the victim with a rope or object.
  4. Do not touch a panicking victim directly.
  5. If unconscious, use a boat or rope to bring the victim to shore.
  6. Remove wet clothes and cover the victim with a blanket.
  7. Seek medical attention for all near-drowning victims.

First Aid for Drowning Victim

✔ DOs

  • Check for hypothermia.
  • Start CPR if the victim is not breathing.
  • Protect the neck and spine.
  • Keep the victim still and calm.
  • Keep the body warm.
  • Provide first aid for other injuries.

✖ DON'Ts

  • Don't enter the water if you are untrained.
  • Don't jump into rough or icy water.
  • Don't use the Heimlich maneuver routinely.
  • Don't delay professional medical treatment.
  • Don't leave the victim alone.

Prevention is the Best Protection

Avoid alcohol while swimming.

Never leave children unattended.

Empty tubs and buckets.

Keep toilet seats covered.

Follow water safety rules.

Learn CPR and water safety.
