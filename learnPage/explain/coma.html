Hepatic Coma

Early recognition and medical treatment are essential

Introduction

Hepatic coma, also called severe hepatic encephalopathy, is a life-threatening condition caused by advanced liver failure. The damaged liver cannot remove harmful substances such as ammonia and other toxins from the blood, allowing them to affect the brain and nervous system.

Symptoms usually develop gradually and may progress from confusion and personality changes to drowsiness, unconsciousness, and coma. Immediate medical attention is required because untreated hepatic coma can be fatal.

Common Causes and Triggers

  • Advanced liver disease and liver failure.
  • Cirrhosis or chronic hepatitis.
  • Internal bleeding from esophageal varices.
  • Infections.
  • Excess protein intake in susceptible patients.
  • Dehydration.
  • Electrolyte imbalance.
  • Constipation.
  • Excessive alcohol intake.
  • Incorrect use of medications such as sedatives or diuretics.

Early Warning Signs

  • Confusion and forgetfulness.
  • Irritability and mood changes.
  • Difficulty concentrating.
  • Sleep disturbances.
  • Slurred speech.
  • Tremors or shaky hands.
  • Poor coordination.
  • Unusual behavior.

Signs of Severe Hepatic Coma

  • Extreme drowsiness.
  • Disorientation.
  • Loss of consciousness.
  • Slow or irregular breathing.
  • Unresponsiveness.
  • Seizures.
  • Coma.
Important: Hepatic coma is a medical emergency. Call emergency medical services immediately.

Immediate First Aid Measures

  1. Call emergency medical services immediately.
  2. Keep the patient resting in a safe position.
  3. Monitor breathing and level of consciousness.
  4. If fully conscious, follow the treatment plan prescribed by the doctor.
  5. Do not give alcohol or unprescribed medicines.
  6. If vomiting occurs, turn the patient onto one side.
  7. If unconscious but breathing, place in the recovery position.
  8. If breathing stops, begin CPR and seek urgent help.

Hospital Treatment

  • Continuous medical monitoring.
  • Identification and treatment of the underlying cause.
  • Management of bleeding or infections.
  • Correction of dehydration and electrolyte imbalance.
  • Administration of lactulose and other medications prescribed by physicians.
  • Special nursing care and nutritional support.
  • Intensive care support in severe cases.
  • Evaluation for liver transplantation when necessary.

Hepatic Coma First Aid

✔ DOs

  • Call emergency services immediately.
  • Keep the patient under observation.
  • Place unconscious patients in the recovery position.
  • Monitor breathing and pulse.
  • Follow the physician's instructions.
  • Inform healthcare professionals about liver disease history.

✖ DON'Ts

  • Do not delay hospital treatment.
  • Do not give alcohol.
  • Do not give food or drinks to unconscious patients.
  • Do not administer medicines without medical advice.
  • Do not leave the patient alone.
  • Do not ignore increasing confusion or drowsiness.

Possible Complications

Without prompt treatment, hepatic coma may lead to permanent brain injury, severe infections, respiratory failure, internal bleeding, multi-organ failure, and death.

Early recognition and rapid medical treatment significantly improve survival and recovery. Any sudden confusion or loss of consciousness in a person with liver disease should always be treated as an emergency.

Prevention and Healthy Habits

💊

Take medicines as prescribed.

🍺

Avoid alcohol consumption.

🥗

Follow dietary advice.

💧

Maintain hydration.

🩺

Attend regular checkups.

🏥

Seek early treatment for liver disease.

Why Immediate Treatment Matters

Hepatic coma is a serious complication of liver failure. Confusion, unusual behavior, increasing sleepiness, and loss of consciousness should never be ignored.

Prompt medical treatment can reverse many cases of hepatic encephalopathy and prevent permanent complications. Early recognition, proper medication, and hospital care play a vital role in improving outcomes.
